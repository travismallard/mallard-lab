Who We Are

A research program in psychiatric and statistical genetics

The Mallard Lab is led by Travis T. Mallard, PhD, Assistant Professor of Psychology in the Department of Psychiatry at Harvard Medical School and Assistant Investigator in Psychiatry at Massachusetts General Hospital. The lab studies the etiology of psychopathology through the lens of genetic architecture, with particular emphasis on externalizing disorders, behavioral disinhibition, comorbidity, and heterogeneity.

Across projects, we integrate large-scale GWAS, multivariate modeling, transcriptomic and neurogenomic annotation, and imaging genetics to map shared and specific liabilities across disorders, identify developmental mechanisms, and build more interpretable tools for genomic discovery.

Active Work

Selected active projects

Current projects span psychiatric genetics, developmental neurogenomics, imaging genetics, and method development, but are unified by a common aim: to explain comorbidity, pleiotropy, and heterogeneity in psychopathology with greater statistical precision and translational value.

Multivariate dissection of externalizing disorders

We are conducting large-scale multivariate genomic studies of externalizing psychopathology to resolve how liability is shared and differentiated across ADHD, conduct disorder, antisocial behavior, substance use disorders, and related dimensions of disinhibition.

Learn more →

Developmental neurogenomics of psychiatric risk

A second line of work examines how genomic liability is expressed across neurodevelopment, including projects on cortical expansion, spatiotemporal gene expression, and the neurodevelopmental mechanisms that connect genomic variation to psychiatric risk.

Learn more →

Methods for multivariate genomic discovery

The lab also develops statistical tools for complex trait genomics, including work related to Genomic SEM and new approaches designed to improve estimation, interpretability, and biologically informed inference in high-dimensional psychiatric genetics.
